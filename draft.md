# Rachel’s Korea Guide

Welcome to Korea! Korea has the most tourists than ever in record. Now, I also met a lot of people visiting my country, I’m glad to guide and give you some local tips

# Tips

- Taking subway during commuting time
    
    Avoid taking subways during commuting time (especially Line 9, 2). Adversely, if you want to know how the extreme commuting in Korea looks like, take Line 9 at Yeouido station ^ㅗ^ (sarcastic smile)
    
- Use Catch Table app
    
    An app to be in queue. Lots of instagram/trendy/popular restaurants adopted the system to manage their waiting list. You can check your number and sometimes you can be in queue remotely. Always check if the restaurant is in the system if you are going to popular ones.
    
    There’s no hidden gem in Seoul. Korean people are sensitive to trends and they are fast changing. If one restaurant is really good, it immediately became viraled and so popular. Considering the massive population in a city, cool/trendy area is always crowded and restaurants always have queue
    
- Maps
    
    Local people uses Naver Map or Kakao Map, instead of Google maps. Using Google maps might give you only touristic recommendations
    
- Open run (오픈런)
    
    This is Konglish which means queuing up early before the restaurants open) Saving seat at the hyped restaurants/cafes are super competitive, people often sprints for them.
    
- Restaurants/Cafes: no tip culture, always provide water, you can ask to refill side dishes, Save seat by putting your stuffs (phones, wallets) on the. sharing food
- Drinking culture: Bar vs Pocha
- Secure society
- 배민
- Are we threatened by North Korea?
    
    They sometimes provokes but (South) Korean doesn’t really feel fear. My foreign friend said “After seeing the biggest Korean fireworks for few years, I have a belief that Korea can defend any attacks”. Indeed, we have a long history (more than thousand years) to be attacked by many surrounding countries and our defense industry is one of the best in the world.

# Shopping

- Daiso (다이소)
    
    You can find the stores every other street. 다이소 sounds like they have everything in Korean (다있어). They have almost everything and super cheap (1000-5000 won). You can find more than 70% of commodities that need in your life. 
    
- Olive Young (올리브영)
    
    Korean skin care shopping, Sephora withdrew their business in Korea because of this drug store.
    
    Most of the products are in high-quality but ask me if you need specific recommendations.
    
- Beauty devices
    
    You can get big discount if you buy it from online duty-free shop
